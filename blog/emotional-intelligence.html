Emotional Intelligence

What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional Intelligence (EI) is the ability to be aware and manage emotions - both our own and others. You can take a standardized test called the Mayer-Salovey-Caruso Emotional Intelligence Test (MSCEIT) which is an ability-based test that measures the four branches of Mayer and Salovey’s El mode.

How is it different to IQ?

Intelligence quotient (IQ) is the measure of a person’s reasoning and ability to problem solve.

EI and IQ differ because EI measures a person’s social and emotional competencies and IQ measures a person’s academic competency and reasoning ability.

A good example would be, someone who has a high IQ level will theoretically have greater success academically in study, where as a person’s EQ level reflects success in the real world.

Why is it important?

Both EQ and IQ are important but I think EQ is more important for the soft skill set in a professional working environment and the real world.

EQ gives you the skill set to recognize and regulate emotion, and to use social awareness in problem-solving. EQ in my opinion is a far greater tool to have than IQ in software development. The soft skill set that comes with EQ allows an individual to work well in a team environment.

IQ gives you the skill set to problem solve which would help immensely being a full stack developer, but if you have a high IQ and low EQ, you wouldn’t be able to communicate well and work in a team environment.

Both intelligence qualities can drastically shape an individuals quality of life and accomplishments.
