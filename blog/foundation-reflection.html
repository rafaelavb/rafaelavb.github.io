Foundation Reflections

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

I’ve learn a whole heap about myself through the Dev Academy journey. The biggest things would have to be how much content I can learn, especially at such a fast rate. When I look back at myself on week 1 with absolutely no coding experience to week 5 have an in depth view into the fundamentals of HTML, CSS and JavaScript I’m quite amazed.

The next thing would be how applying the concepts of a growth mindset changed how I would view my learning through Foundations.

The last thing that I learnt about myself was that I could infant learn to code after spending 10 years in the trade.

What are the role of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

When you’re programming and working In a team, these certain soft skills make you a great and valued team member. Most of these soft skills will be learnt to some extent through everyday life. By studying and understanding the core logic to these soft skills, you’re able to be mindful and make more conscious decisions in programming as a whole.

Values are what an individual believes in, It is what is most important in someones life. An individuals core values guides them through the way they live and work. They determine ones priorities and actions. Everyone's core values are different but that’s what makes people unique. I think having good core values allows someone to work well in a team environment and would carry them through a successful career in software development.

Empathy allows someone to emotionally understand what other people feel. Working in a team environment means working with multiple other people, all who will manage their jobs while juggling different lives behind the scenes. People live dynamic lives and within that, things do happen. A good example would be Covid19. By practising empathy, people can build empathetic connections which enhance relationships and performance.

Practising self-awareness allows us to become better decision makers. By being self-aware and thinking before speaking, we are able to communicate with more clarity and intention. It allows an individual to understand things from multiple perspectives rather than narrowing in on one. It can allow an individual to listen without bias or assumption. This is advantageous while working in software and contributing as an active team member.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

I’m the most surprised at how much of the core learning I’ve already been practising in my daily life. I’m surprised that the core learning has made me more aware of soft skills that will beneficial to me within a software environment and through life in general. Having the core learning element in Dev Academy is a great way to help people grow internally.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

I found the most challenging aspects for me would have to be actually publishing the blogs themselves. The thought of people I don’t know possibly reading my blogs, thoughts and plans was a new concept to me. I’ve never really posted too much on social media and nothing this personal before. The other hard part was deciding how much do I actually want to add in my blog about myself and my thoughts. How can I convey myself in such a way that I’m happy with my post and then uploading it to my blog.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

Dev Academy is focusing heavily on the core learning aspect because companies would rather hire a junior developer with a high level of key soft skills and a willingness to advance their coding. As a junior developer you will be reaching out daily for guidance and clarity and that is why is it so crucial that you are a good fit. A junior developer who is able to fit nicely into a team is more valued than someone who is a high level programmer with no team skills.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practise programming instead? Justify your answer.

I think the time I spent developing and learning the core side to Dev Academy has been rewarding. I can continue to learn coding through Codecademy, after boot camp and through employment as a junior software developer. I’m glad I was able to improve on my core skills while learning to code so that I can become a more well rounded team member. The material I have learnt through Dev Academy has become a vital part of me transitioning from an HVAC Mechanic to a Software Developer.
