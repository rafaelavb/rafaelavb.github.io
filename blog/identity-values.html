Explain a situation where you have had an ethical decision to make. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and the consequences of your action.

A situation where I have had to make an ethical decision was when I was an apprentice in the HVAC industry, I was told to de-gas some old heat pumps taken out of a retirement block. I began use a reclaim cylinder (like an LPG bottle to recover gas) and a reclaim unit (a machine to pull all the gas out) at the workshop when my boss walked down the stairs and told me that there wasn’t enough time to use the machine and to make a small hole in the heat pumps and let the gas vent out into the atmosphere.

I was a new apprentice and quite fresh at this stage and was eager to please but on the other hand, to vent an HFC refrigerant gas is illegal and destroys the ozone layer.

The decision I made was to in fact work later than expected. Carry out using the reclaim machine and cylinder to legally store the gas so It cant get properly destroyed in a facility.

The consequences of my action was for a decent amount of my apprenticeship, I did feel like a got a bit of a hard time from the specific manager but at the end of the day I did the right thing. I have now been in the same trade for 10 years and to this day I’ve never vented refrigerant gas but know many people who have done so to save time.

Describe how your culture has influenced your values and identity.

I come from quite a diverse culture of Latin American and Dutch heritage. From a young age, core values like family, honesty and determination were instilled into me by my family.

A good example of family would be, on September the 18th, all my South American family gather at a house and celebrate national Chilean independence day. We all make traditional dishes accustom to Chile and work together to make Chilean empanadas. This event has been taken place since my family moved to New Zealand.

We also share a slightly difference Christmas. We all gather on Christmas Eve, have a big pot luck and open all our presents at mid night. I then spend the rest of Christmas day with my other side of the family and reflecting on the year.

Evaluate your strengths and limitations in terms of your learning and career development

A strength of mine that I am proud of is my work ethic, and attention to detail. I take a lot of pride in my work, and thoroughly enjoy providing professional advice, solving problems, and coming up with innovative solutions. I can be a perfectionist to a fault, but I always enjoy giving things my best effort.

Something I have always struggled with which limits me is a work-life balance. When I left school, I joined the trades and buckled down to save for a house. I lived very frugally, and achieved my goal. When I bought the house, I started my own business in HVAC. Although running your own business comes with its benefits, I am never able to “switch off” due to having to respond to emails/calls around the clock, and I working throughout the week and weekends.

It has come with a lot of ongoing stress, and I find that when I am not at work, I am always thinking about work/doing paperwork. As work has both busy and quiet periods, I feel that you cannot say no to when it comes up, including weekends.

By not achieving a good work-life balance, I felt constantly burnt out which halted forms of career development. Having a better structured work-life balance is one of the reasons I am pursuing a career change in software development.

Identify which of your strengths might help you in your learning journey and how they intersect with learning obstacles.

I believe the Dev Academy online cohort foundations core module is built on personal development. By being able to look more into myself and ask some real questions about who I am, what I believe in, and what do I want out of this course, I feel that it is helping me to become a better person.

From the start of the course, there has been a lot of cooperation and team building. Getting to know your peers with certain tasks designed around introducing yourself. Once the boot camp starts, we will have to code in peers and work as teams to build and create products. I’ve been working in teams in my professional working career most of my life. Being a tradie involves most days working in teams, whether it be with colleagues or other trades. Everyone has to tie in with their job to get to finish the job. In my career, jobs can be as little as a day or up to one and a half years. I think having the core value of cooperation with benefit me well by working in a team environment, though my Dev Academy experience and into my first job as a junior developer.

Share an example from your experience of where you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss the strategies you used at that time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why?

There has been a few jobs I’ve been on when there has been tension among some of the trades working closely together because plans haven’t been followed correctly and there has been clashes of where certain products/ materials can and cant be installed.

The resistance happened when the team I was working with were installing cassette heat pumps into a ceiling of a new build retirement village.

We couldn’t install 3 cassette heat pumps in the ceiling due to the sparkies running there cables right through the middle of where they were planned to go. We informed the sparkies and it caused some tension as they said they weren’t going to move their cables. This caused quite a problem because if we didn’t get our heat pumps in the right place, the ceiling wouldn’t line up properly.

In the end, we went over our plans and checked that they were up to date before pushing the matter further. Once we had determined our plans were up to date, we asked the sparkies to see their plans and found they were out dated.

After bringing this to the sparkies attention, they agreed to move the cables.

Reflecting back on this situation, I feel the right steps were followed and were effective at getting to the end goal. Other strategies could have involved informing the site foreman or one of our general managers overseeing our part of the job and letting them sort out the issue. This would have let us complete another task and carry on being productive.

From a trade perspective, I don’t think it could have been handled differently and I wouldn’t try any different strategy at that point of resistance. The situation was deescalated quickly and effectively. If we had taken the matter to the site foreman or one of our mangers, the result would have, in my opinion, caused some bad blood between the trades and wouldn't have allowed both teams to work together amicably on this long project.
