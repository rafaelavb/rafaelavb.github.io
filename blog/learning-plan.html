Learning Plan

What is your long term goal or career pathway?

My long term career pathway would be to find a job as a junior developer with a company that has a clear pathway, and enables me opportunities to further my learning. I want to be able to become an intermediate developer within 2-3 years. When I feel more competent and independent with my skill set, I would like to start traveling and freelance on the side.

A description of your strengths and limitations concerning learning

I am persistent, curious, committed and determined when it comes to learning something new.

I have a huge motivational drive, and throw myself into work presented to me.These strengths enable me to deep-dive into new topics/concepts, and learn large amounts of information in a short period of time.

However, when I immerse myself quickly, I find myself over-committing and over-working. This can quickly lead to me losing track of time, and putting too much effort into a single piece of work rather than moving on or allowing myself a break.

What skills (non-technical - human skills) would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

During my time a Dev Academy, I’d like to see my time management and prioritization skills improve.

Although I am new to coding, I have been able to develop a variety of soft skills due to my time and experience in the trade. Some examples of these soft skills include communication, organization and cooperation.

However, completing the exercises and answering these questions of reflection set by Dev Academy, I have found that I struggle to prioritize time and my time management of tasks.

A commitment to how you will manage your workload in this programme.

Routine, routine, routine.

I am beginning to change my routine immensely to fit my needs for Dev Academy.

Investing this amount of time can be very damaging without proper routine and structure. I know when Boot Camp ramps up, it can require 60-70 hours per week to complete the criteria. I've taken steps to accommodate this.

Working as an HVAC mechanic for the past 10 years, I’ve gotten used to being on my feet for the majority of the day. Because of this, I’ve brought a sit/stand desk to try maintain part of my old working environment.

I’ve made a study nook in my house which is bright, quiet and tidy. I rent some rooms in my house to my friends, but they are supportive and aware of my time commitments to Dev Academy. They are respectful during the times I am doing my coursework.

When I hit a block and find myself spending too much time on a task needing help, my first option would be to use my good mate Google. The downside to using Google is that due to being quite green in software development, I don’t actually know some of the jargon used. You don’t know what you don’t know, so sometimes I don’t actually know what to search for to help me with my issue. This can send me down the rabbit hole, and suck up a lot of time.

Discord would be my next option as chances are, someone else in one of the cohorts has already run into the same problem.

If I fail to find a solution through these avenues, I’d then put out a message to one of the facilitators for some guidance.

I’m sure many of the facilitation team have trained plenty of cohorts before me. Because of their experience with many others, I would expect them to have amazing communication skills, problem solving skills, empathy, and guidance. They have already helped me keep on track with the Monday zoom meetings, and when I have needed their help when I have gotten stuck.
