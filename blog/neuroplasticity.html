Neuroplasticity

Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people

By understanding neuroplasticity, you understand the scientific backed evidence that the brain is malleable and that you can change brain cells in response to your behavior through the course of your life. The more active we are at engaging and challenging our mind and body, the longer our brain will function at a higher level. These responses can benefit people by allowing us to more thoughtfully engage in activities/life that will contribute to our well being.

Discuss how you might engage with the principles of neuroplasticity for your benefit

There are many ways to engage with the principles. In my opinion, you first must actually believe in and be open to the concept. Becoming engaged in neuroplasticity requires you to pick a skill that should be new to you. It should be relatively hard and you should show some interest in it. Practising the skill allows you to engage the principles of neuroplasticity.

This could involve:
Learning a new language
Learning a new instrument
Cooking new recipes
Traveling
Practising mindfulness

What are some of the ways to increase your neuroplasticity?

You can increase neuroplasticity in a number of ways.

Firstly, you need to think of your brain as a muscle. The more you exercise your brain correctly the more powerful it ill become.

Your brain can use up to a quarter of what you eat so having a balanced healthy diet rich in Vitamin D and magnesium can promote neuroplasticity. Research shows that having between 7-9 hours sleep with a 20 minute nap in the afternoon can promote your neuroplasticity even higher. Creating downtime and creating an “end of the day” ritual that includes reflection and gratitude boosts endorphins which creates perfect conditions for neuroplasticty.

Other tasks like using the non-dominant hand forms new neural pathways and will strengthen connectivity between existing neurons.

Discuss what it is and why it is relevant

A growth mindset is a form of view that your abilities are changeable and something you can improve through with exercise and practise.

Growth mindset factors:
You see failure as a chance to learn and pivot
See critical feedback as a chance to improve and develop
More likely to embrace challenges and tackle them head on working hard to improve
View obstacles as a chance to experiment and solve problem

Mindset influences everything from creative risk-taking to how you view feedback or whether or not you finish difficult tasks. Having a growth mindset is one of the biggest factors in determining whether or not you grow and improve in your abilities.
