Problem Solving

Were you blocked on a simple problem?

In sprint one when I was learning HTML and CSS, I got stuck trying to format my index page. The index page to my blog kept expanding higher than the screen limit and you would have to scroll out to 40% zoom to see the top of the page on a screen. I kept going through the code, using the process of elimination with the comment out function to no avail. Many hours passed and many breaks were taken before I gave up and accepted my fate. People were just going to have to zoom out on my front page if they wanted to see it. Around 4 day passed of me pretending there wasn’t an issue before I decided to dive back into the code. I decided to add a nav bar and when I was mucking around with the css, I scrolled up to the top of my file and saw I had added a value which referenced the height of the front page which made it blow out to around 3 screens high. I deleted the line and the whole page fell into place.

Did you elegantly solved a problem?

A problem I elegantly solved would have been all of the JS Intro Conditionals. I really struggled with with JS Intro as a whole. I’m extremely green at coding and was having issues with my conditionals until I figured out how Dev Academy wanted me to structure the answers. I was over complicating everything. Once I figured that part out I used the math I learnt in school and breezed through that part. I felt pretty pleased to breeze though that leg to give me more time on other parts of JS Intro. In both cases, I’ve learnt not to sweat the small stuff and look at the bigger picture. If I compare myself at the start of sprint 1 to the start of sprint 4, I’ve learnt a heap of new things in such small space of time.
